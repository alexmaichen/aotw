# Aspect of the Week : :magnet: Talos Fists :fist:
On the surface, the idea behind Talos appears to make sense to go fast with: surely, pulling enemies towards the player must help with reducing movement! The problem with this is that first of all the special-animation on this fist is a bit slower than those by Zagfists or Demeter (unless you interrupt it with a dash of course, which only pulls the enemy towards you without triggering the special), and is no stronger than a special from any other fist.

Additionally, it only pulls one enemy towards you, meaning this idea would not benefit at all from enemy-grouping through spawn-manipulation (hell, it would be actively detrimental if used in that way, as it splits the group into 2). Because of this, here's another piece of tech: if you special and no enemy is available to pull in that direction, the magnet will not be sent out, allowing you to more quickly deploy the special or dash-upper, which hopefully will linger around for long enough to hit a currently spawning enemy.

As usual for fists, the preferred builds are :MercifulEnd: or ZAP.

The [unmodded](<https://www.speedrun.com/hades/runs/yvvd8qoy>) and [modded](<https://www.speedrun.com/hades/runs/z19g1ejm>) WR are held by ghosty and zyru respectively. Ghosty used ZAP, zyru used ME.

## for heat
Talos is a bit of a quirky fist heat-spect. It has three viable starting hammers: Long Knuckle, Breaching Cross and Heavy Knuckle.

As for boons, it usually wants the normal fist zeus attack, as well as various tier 2s, like any other fist, or most rails. There is a more experimental build-path involving heavy knuckle, a high-%/artemis attack, and either artemis' support fire boon or a damaging dash. Build-options for this aspect have been very well-explored, with a few unconventional build-options being available; though no more formal guides exist for these as of right now (outside of past AotW writeups).

Both the [unmodded (56)](<https://www.twitch.tv/videos/2134511569>) and [modded (58)](<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mC51CzzlHyY&t=37s>) world records are held by Scratch.
